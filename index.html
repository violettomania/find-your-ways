Our tours

Crash course Budapest

A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step – the essentials of Budapest every visitor has to know. General orientation on our history, culture, everyday life, dos and don'ts in 2 to 4 hours.

Sights featured: Parliament, Buda Castle, Fisherman's Bastion, Market hall, Heroes square, St Stephen's Basilica, and many more.

Mysterious 7 – Design above all

In the 7th district art is literally all around. Murals make the walls colorful. Boutique stores everywhere filled with indie designers' goods, clothes, accessories. It doesn’t matter if you prefer vintage items or highest quality products money can buy - here you’ll find something that steals your heart.

After a walk in this exciting neighborhood a ruin bar with its unique atmosphere is the best place to recharge your batteries. We finish the tour in a silkscreening shrine where you can learn and try the basics of silk screening on zero waste materials, and take home the wearables you created.

Mysterious 7 – Everything about Hungarian Jews...

...you always wanted to know, but were afraid to ask! The roller-coaster history of the Hungarian Jews is intriguing. And it's all condensed in Budapest’s 7th district. In the past 200 years the Hungarian Jews were privileged enough to be able to build Europe's largest synagogue and damned enough that 80% of them was killed during WW2. Today the Jewish district is a thriving cultural hub again. What's going on here? This is what we explore.

Half ways down the tour we stop for a coffee and ‘flódni’ - a traditional Hungarian-Jewish cake. (Optional visit to the Dohány street synagogue complex on request.)

Mysterious 7 – Jewish deluxe

First we do a complete tour of the Dohány synagogue complex (synagogue main part, museum, Tree of Life, Raoul Wallenberg memorial park. Then a backstage visit inside synagogue with the rabbi's daughter. She shows us hidden corners and closed parts of this beautiful synagogue. Available with special permission from Rabbi Frőlich.

What have the Romans ever done for us?

It's a cliche that Hungary is always on the border of East and West. But hell it's true! Check any map from 2000 years ago, you'll see everything was the same. Ruins of ancient Roman provincial capital Aquincum is still attractive. Its invaluable findings eg a 1800 years old organ and the reconstructed buildings of a shrine of Mithras with the painter's house introduce you to the everyday life, fears and beliefs of the Romans. This tour is the best on the wheels.

Hungarian folk revival

Enough of “Made in China souvenirs”? Want to take a peek behind “Goulash”? Discover the authentic heritage and folk art. Try traditional musical instruments, be amazed by the intricate embroideries, get on the loom and let it roll. The richness of articulation and craftsmanship of rural Hungarian folk art is truly amazing.

Thanks to a bunch of youngsters in the 70's who ventured to remote corners of the Carpathian basin to collect and save the heritage of a disappearing traditional lifestyle. They mastered the techniques and brought it back to the city. The young urban guys are grey-haired university professors and teachers now. The old dancers, singers, musicians and craftsmen are long gone, but their heritage is passed on. A folk revival was born. Today it is more vivid than ever. The most tactile tour in town.

Eco-walk

Sustainability. Social responsibility. Do these words mean anything? What do we do in our everyday lives to protect the environment? Can we do anything at all? On this tour we see examples how we Hungarians act for our future. From a 0.0 energy consumption kindergarten to a zero waste garment designer, we stop at a café dedicated to the integration of people with disabilities and many more. Optionally you can make your own wearables on our silk screening workshop.

Transport provided by GreenGo electric carsharing.

From liberal prince to illiberal king

Hungarian prime minister Viktor Orbán has a rather polarizing reputation in foreign press. Is he a threat or a new hope for Europe? Which one is he? Neither? Both? You can decide once you see the big picture. One thing is for sure. He's been in business for 30 years. Through his story you can learn a lot from Hungary and the Hungarians. Not only that, but it also sheds a light on the conflicts and successes Central Europe has gone through.

Regardless of your political preferences it's a sensitive topic. This tour is not about judging, but understanding. Get prepared for a big walk with a lot of archive footage, controversial stories, famous and infamous places.
